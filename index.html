Welcome to my home page!

I develop computational frameworks to study complex systems that arise from large-scale interactions across domains such as infectious disease dynamics, socio-environmental resilience, agriculture, and social systems. My work draws on and contributes to network science, simulation modeling, algorithm design, and machine learning. Central to my research are the development of realistic synthetic datasets (like digital twins) and data-driven simulation models, the design of domain-informed algorithms, and the advancement of theoretical foundations underlying these complex systems.

Much of my work takes place in interdisciplinary team environments and has been supported by multiple funding agencies. I have led USDA- and USAID-funded projects that address real-world socio-technical challenges.
