Welcome to my home page!

I develop computational frameworks to study various real-world problems that can be characterized by complex interactions between multiple entities. My prominent application areas include public health, agriculture and social sciences. With my current research centered around the science and engineering of network dynamical systems, I construct realistic synthetic datasets (like digital twins) and simulation models, apply machine learning & optimization algorithms for various computational problems that arise in the target applications, and contribute to the theoretical foundations of these systems. My work, often carried out in interdisciplinary team environments, has been supported by multiple agencies over the years, including leadership roles in USDA- and USAID-funded projects. Broadly, my research spans the following areas in computational sciences: network science, data analytics, algorithm design, combinatorics, machine learning, and game theory.
