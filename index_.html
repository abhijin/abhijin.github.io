abhijin

Broadly, my interests are in the development and application of computation tools for real-world problems. My current focus is on the science and engineering of simulation systems with application to public health, agriculture, etc. where I have the unique oppotunity to work at the intersection of domain-specific problems, data, theoretical foundations, and engineering digital twins and simulation systems. I apply tools from network science, data analytics, algorithm design, combinatorics, machine learning, and game theory. My current focus is on the foundations and applications of dynamical systems on networks with application to public health and resilience in agriculture. There are three main thrusts to my research. Through large collaborative efforts, I design and analyze data- and process-driven models using domain-specific knowledge in the context of complex phenomena such as infectious disease spread and biological invasions. Secondly, I strive to advance the theoretical foundations of such models to create robust models that can be applied in data-poor environments. Thirdly, I develop efficient practical solutions with provable guarantees for various computational and learning problems that arise in the target applications.
